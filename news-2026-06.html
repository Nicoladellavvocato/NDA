MISSION UPDATES · ISSUE 01 · JUN 2026

Liftoff.

Why this newsletter exists, the ISS tracker goes online, and what's next.

Welcome to Mission Updates — an occasional dispatch about what I'm building in aerospace, mission analysis and flight dynamics. Short, technical, no noise.

01 Why this exists

I wanted one place to share the things that don't fit in a CV line: a simulation I'm running, a build that finally flew, a result from the lab. If you signed up from the site — thank you. You'll hear from me only when there's something worth sending.

02 What's new

The ISS live tracker is online on the site: it reads live orbital data, computes the station's sub-point and plots its position and trail over a world map in real time. It grew out of my work computing satellite positions in Python with skyfield (TLE / SGP4), rebuilt as a small, robust in-browser component.

03 What's next

I'm finishing my BSc in aerospace engineering at Politecnico di Torino and heading toward mission analysis and space-systems work. Coming up: write-ups on trajectory simulation with RocketPy, and notes from my thesis on fibre-optic structural health monitoring of composites.
